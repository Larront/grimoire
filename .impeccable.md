# .impeccable.md

Design context for Grimoire — read by impeccable skills before any UI work.

## Design Context

### Users

Tabletop RPG game masters managing lore, NPCs, maps, and session prep for their campaigns. They use Grimoire in focused creative sessions — building worlds between sessions, or reviewing notes right before running one. The mindset is a mix of creative flow and practical organization. They need to find things quickly, capture ideas without friction, and feel like the tool understands what they're doing.

### Brand Personality

**Three words:** Arcane, Grounded, Purposeful

The name "Grimoire" is the north star — a spellbook of record, not a game or a toy. The interface should feel like a well-crafted creative instrument: special enough to feel distinct from a generic productivity app, restrained enough that the content (the world itself) always takes center stage.

Tone: quiet confidence. Not loud or decorative. The fantasy aesthetic lives in the typography and color choices, not in iconography or ornament.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Primary:** Dark mode. This is the canonical experience. Light mode is secondary. All design decisions are evaluated first in the dark palette (dark navy `oklch(0.148 0.004 228.8)` background, teal primary).

**Typography:** `Metamorphous` (headings) + `Nunito` (body). This pairing is load-bearing for the aesthetic — Metamorphous provides the arcane, codex-like character; Nunito keeps body text warm and readable. Lean into this contrast deliberately.

**Color:** Cool-toned. The teal primary and dark navy are not warm fantasy browns — they read as moonlight, deep water, the glow of a torch in stone. Avoid warm amber/gold decorative color unless it appears in content.

**References:** Somewhere between Obsidian (focused, dark, content-first) and a beautifully typeset physical sourcebook. Not a digital dungeon map. Not a corporate wiki.

**Anti-references:**
- No generic SaaS or dashboard aesthetic (no Notion-style maximalism, no Linear-style cold minimalism)
- No gamified or kitsch dungeon-crawler visuals (no pixel art, no excessive fantasy iconography)
- Not so minimal that personality disappears — the design should have a point of view
- Not cluttered — panels, toolbars, and widgets should not compete for attention

### Design Principles

1. **The world, not the tool.** UI chrome should recede. Every sidebar item, button, and border earns its presence by serving the content — the lore, the maps, the scenes — not the interface itself.

2. **Arcane clarity.** Fantasy character is expressed through typography (`Metamorphous` headings), color depth, and subtle texture — never through literal fantasy decoration. A heading should feel like a chapter title in a sourcebook.

3. **Dark by nature.** Dark mode is canon. Evaluate contrast, depth, and richness in the dark palette first. Light mode follows, it does not lead.

4. **Purposeful restraint.** Personality comes from a few deliberate, well-executed choices — not from accumulation. One strong typographic moment is better than five decorative ones.

5. **GM clarity under pressure.** GMs often need to find information fast, mid-session. Information hierarchy must be scannable at a glance. Labels should be unambiguous. Loading states should be minimal and non-disruptive.
